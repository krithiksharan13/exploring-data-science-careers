## Poster Structure

### Introduction
Defines what a data scientist is, combining statistics, machine learning, and domain expertise to drive business decisions. Highlights the breadth of the field and its accessibility to graduates from varied academic backgrounds.

### Section 1: Industries & Demand
Explores the wide range of sectors actively hiring data scientists, including tech (Google, Amazon), finance (HSBC, Goldman Sachs), healthcare (AstraZeneca, NHS), retail (Alibaba, Walmart), and consulting (Deloitte, PwC).

### Section 2: Skills & Qualifications
Breaks down the technical skills required (Python, R, SQL, statistics, machine learning) alongside the often-overlooked importance of soft skills (communication, problem-solving, business understanding). Covers undergraduate and postgraduate routes, certifications, and the value of practical projects and Kaggle competitions.

### Section 3: Career Progression & Salaries
Maps out the flexible career pathway from entry-level roles (£25,000–£35,000) through mid-level (£40,000–£60,000), into technical specialist tracks (£60,000–£90,000+) and leadership roles (£90,000–£120,000+), up to executive level positions such as Chief Data Officer (£290,000+).

### Section 4: Finding a Job
Covers the main job search methods: networking and LinkedIn, online job platforms, direct company applications, recruiters, GitHub portfolios, and university graduate schemes.

### Conclusion
Summarises the three pillars of a successful data science career: quantitative education, continuously updated technical skills, and strong communication abilities. Reinforces data science as one of the most resilient and rewarding graduate careers available today.

---

## Key Findings

- Data science spans virtually every major industry and is not limited to tech
- Soft skills are equally as important as technical proficiency
- Career paths are flexible, offering both deep specialist and senior leadership routes
- Salary progression is strong at every stage, reflecting sustained market demand
- Practical experience (projects, competitions, portfolios) increasingly differentiates candidates
